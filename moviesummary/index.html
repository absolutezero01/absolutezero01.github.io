The Interview

 Genre: Comedy
Director: Seth Rogen, Evan Goldberg
Writers: Dan Sterling
Dave Skylark (and his producer Aaron Rapoport run the popular celebrity tabloid TV show Skylark Tonight. When they discover that North Korean dictator Kim Jong-un is a fan of the show, they land an interview with him in an attempt to legitimize themselves as journalists. As Dave and Aaron prepare to travel to Pyongyang, their plans change when the CIA recruits them, perhaps the two least-qualified men imaginable to assassinate Kim Jong-un.

My reaction to the movie

Most of the jokes in the interview are childish, but this is fine because it is meant for a late teen/early adult public that likes R Rated comedies and satirical entertainment filled with pop-culture references (such as Rick and Morty, Family Guy and South Park). There were quite a few cheap gag moments and 'bro humor' but I'd be lying if I said I didn't enjoy them. James Franco as Dave Skylark has a very childish character. His immature traits are very exaggerated. Seth Rogen as his show's director is much more mature of the two. The Interview is primarily at its best when presenting an unabashedly silly parody of North Korea and its dictator.

I was actually excited when they first announced this movie. I was sad when they cancelled to release the movie just because some North Korean hackers tried to blackmail the Sony Entertainment's corporate leaders.
